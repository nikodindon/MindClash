## YouTube description

> If Jean-Marc Jancovici is the high priest of energy realism, Amory Lovins
> is its most persuasive heretic. A physicist, the founder of the Rocky
> Mountain Institute, and the man who literally coined the term "negawatt",
> Lovins has spent 50 years arguing that we don't need new power plants —
> we need to stop wasting the ones we already have.
>
> In this second episode of MindClash, we took 4 of his most influential
> talks — TED, Reinventing Fire, TEDx Applied Hope, and his RMI keynote —
> and we extracted the core of his optimistic vision. With Marc, who
> really studied them, we walk through negawatts, soft energy paths,
> design integratif, and the controversial claim that **efficiency is ten
> times cheaper than nuclear at cutting CO₂**.
>
> In this episode:
> - The "fifth option" : why the fossil/nuclear/climate trilemma is a false choice
> - Negawatts : the cheapest, cleanest power plant is the one you don't build
> - Design integratif : why Empire State Building cut its energy use by 70%
> - Soft energy paths : the 1976 manifesto still relevant in 2026
> - An honest critique : where Lovins is right, where the optimism gets thin
>
> 🎙️ With Alex (host) and Marc (expert)
